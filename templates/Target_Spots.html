Target spots is a fungal disease that affects the cotton boll whhich is caused by Corynespora cassiicola. It can cause brown lesions.Target spot can reduce cotton yield in severe cases, especially in humid and rainy conditions.

Treatment :

  • growers can use resistant or tolerant cultivars.
  • apply fungicides such as Headline or Quadris during the third and fifth weeks of bloom.
  • Fungicides should be applied with drop-nozzles to reach the interior of the canopy, where the disease is most prevalent.
